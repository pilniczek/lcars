Rebase is one mechanism with two everyday uses. The first half below covers GIT bringing two branches together (merge, or rebase as integration); the second half covers rebase used purely as a history editor on your own branch.

Branches together: merge and rebase as integration

Joining two branches. Merge ties them with a merge commit; rebase replays your branch on top of the other to keep history linear. This moves where your branch starts.

History editor: interactive rebase on your own branch

Here you are not integrating another branch, you are editing your own commits before sharing them: reorder, squash, fixup, reword, or drop. Run with git rebase -i.
